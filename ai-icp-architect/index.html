01

The ICP Excavator

Surfaces your best existing clients as the model for future ones

What you're doing: Mining your existing client base for patterns. Your best clients are already telling you exactly who to target. You just need to listen.

ROLE: B2B growth strategist specialising in ICP definition for founder-led businesses.

TASK: Analyse the client inputs below and identify the single highest-value ICP.

INPUTS:
Best 3 clients (describe each): [Company size / industry / what they hired you for / result delivered]
Worst-fit client you've worked with: [why it was hard]
Your current average deal value: [€/£/$]

OUTPUT a structured ICP Profile with these fields:
- Role/title (exact job title, not department)
- Company stage (revenue, headcount, years in business)
- Industry vertical (specific, not "B2B")
- Primary pain (1 sentence: the thing keeping them up at night)
- Vanity metric they care about (the number they're measured on)
- What they've already tried (and why it didn't work)

RULES:
- Be specific. No generalisations.
- Use the language a founder would actually use, not HR-speak.
- 6 fields only. No chain of thought.
OUTPUT: A structured ICP Profile built from your real client patterns
02

The Trigger Mapper

Identifies the moments your ICP becomes ready to buy

What you're doing: Finding the trigger events that move your ICP from passive interest to active buying. These are the signals you'll watch for on LinkedIn.

ROLE: B2B sales strategist. Buying signals are your speciality.

TASK: Using the ICP Profile from Prompt 1, identify the 5 most common trigger events that push this ICP into the market for a solution like mine.

For each trigger:
- Name the event (e.g. "hired a new Head of Sales")
- Explain why it creates urgency
- Describe how it shows up on LinkedIn (what they post, comment on, or search for)

RULES:
- Triggers must be observable: things you can detect from public behaviour
- Focus on events that indicate near-term buying intent (within 0–90 days)
- 5 triggers only. Table format: Trigger | Why Urgent | LinkedIn Signal
OUTPUT: 5 observable buying triggers + LinkedIn signals to watch for
03

The Voice Extractor

Captures the exact language your ICP uses to describe their problem

What you're doing: Harvesting the words your ICP already uses. The best copy doesn't sound clever. It sounds familiar. Use this output to write every post, email, and CTA.

ROLE: B2B copywriter and customer research specialist.

TASK: Using the ICP Profile and trigger events above, generate a Voice of Customer (VoC) dictionary for this specific ICP.

OUTPUT these 4 sections:
1. PAIN PHRASES: 5 ways this ICP describes their core problem in their own words (conversational, not polished)
2. ASPIRATION PHRASES: 5 ways they describe what success looks like to them
3. OBJECTION PHRASES: 3 things they say when they're not ready to act
4. DECISION LANGUAGE: 3 phrases that signal they've moved from curious to committed

RULES:
- Write as if you're quoting them directly
- Use first-person voice ("I", "we", "our")
- No marketing language. These are internal thoughts, not elevator pitches.
OUTPUT: A VoC dictionary to power every piece of content and outreach you create
