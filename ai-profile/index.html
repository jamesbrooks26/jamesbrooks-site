The problem

Your LinkedIn profile is losing you business

Most B2B founders treat their LinkedIn profile like a CV. A list of roles. A summary of services. A timeline of where they have been.

The problem is that your ideal prospect does not care about where you have been. They care about one thing: whether you can solve their problem. If your profile does not make that clear in the first five seconds, they are gone.

This workflow rewrites your profile from a passive CV into an active sales asset. One that works while you sleep, attracts the right founders, and filters out the wrong ones.

The Profile Paradox: Your profile is the most visited page in your entire sales process, and the one founders spend the least time optimising.

Every connection request you send, every comment you leave, every post that lands, all of it drives profile traffic. And most of that traffic arrives, scans three seconds of your headline, and leaves. Not because they are not interested. Because your profile was built to impress, not to convert. It tells people what you do. It does not tell them what they get.

What you'll walk away with
  • A rewritten LinkedIn headline that stops the right founders mid-scroll
  • An About section that converts profile visitors into inbound connection requests
  • A Featured section strategy that turns your best content into a lead generation engine
  • A Services section that replaces vague descriptions with specific, outcome-driven copy
  • A complete profile audit score so you know exactly what to fix first

01

The Profile Auditor

Scores each section of your profile and identifies the single biggest conversion problem in each

What you're doing: Getting an honest, structured assessment of what your profile is currently costing you. Start here. Always audit before you edit.

ROLE: LinkedIn conversion specialist. Your only metric is whether a profile converts the right prospects into inbound connections.

TASK: Audit the LinkedIn profile content I provide below. Score each section out of 10 and identify the single biggest problem in each.

SECTIONS TO AUDIT:
- Headline
- About / Summary
- Featured section
- Services section
- Experience section (first entry only)

INPUTS:
Current headline: [paste your current headline]
Current About section: [paste your current About section]
Featured section: [describe what you currently have here]
Services listed: [list your current services]
First experience entry: [paste your most recent role and description]

RULES:
- Score each section 1–10
- Identify the single biggest conversion problem per section
- Flag any jargon that means nothing to a cold prospect
- Flag any missing outcome statements
- Output: a table with Section | Score | Biggest Problem
- No solutions yet. Audit only.
OUTPUT: A section-by-section audit table with scores and the biggest conversion problem in each area
02

The Headline Rewriter

Builds the most important line on your entire profile

What you're doing: Rewriting the one line that every visitor reads first. Your headline appears in search results, connection requests, comment sections and DMs. It is doing more work than any other line you write.

ROLE: B2B copywriter. LinkedIn headline conversion is your only focus.

TASK: Rewrite the LinkedIn headline using the audit from Prompt 1 and the inputs below. Generate three headline options, each under 220 characters.

INPUTS:
ICP from AI ICP Architect: [paste your ICP profile]
Core outcome you deliver: [the specific result your best clients get]
Your differentiator: [what makes your approach different]
Tone preference: [direct / authoritative / peer-to-peer]

RULES:
- Lead with who you help and what they get, not what you do
- No jargon, no buzzwords, no "passionate about"
- At least one option must include a specific number or outcome
- Output: three numbered options with a one-line rationale for each
OUTPUT: Three headline options with rationale, ready to test
03

The About Section Writer

Converts your profile summary into an inbound conversion engine

What you're doing: Writing the section most founders either skip or fill with career history. Done right, the About section is the highest-converting piece of copy on your entire profile.

ROLE: B2B copywriter. Your one metric is inbound connection requests from ICPs.

TASK: Write a LinkedIn About section using the 5-part structure below.

STRUCTURE:
1. Hook (1 sentence): The problem your ICP is living with right now
2. Agitation (1–2 sentences): What happens if they do not fix it
3. Your solution (2–3 sentences): What you do and what outcome it creates
4. Proof (1–2 sentences): A specific result with a number, timeframe, or client type
5. CTA (1 sentence): Tell them exactly what to do next

INPUTS:
ICP: [who specifically you help]
Core problem: [the biggest problem they face right now]
Your solution: [what you do and how]
Best result: [your strongest client result with a number if possible]
CTA: [what you want them to do: connect, DM, visit link]

RULES:
- No career history or CV narrative
- No first-person opening: do not start with "I"
- Every sentence must earn its place
- Output: the full About section, ready to paste
OUTPUT: A complete About section ready to paste directly into LinkedIn
04

The Featured Section Strategist

Turns your best content into a lead generation engine

What you're doing: Deciding exactly what to feature and why. Most founders either leave this blank or use it as a portfolio. It should be doing active conversion work on every profile visit.

ROLE: LinkedIn conversion strategist. The Featured section is your most underused sales asset.

TASK: Design a Featured section strategy using the inputs below. Recommend exactly what to include in each of the four featured slots and write a short description for each item.

INPUTS:
Lead magnets available: [list your downloadable resources, if any]
Best performing post: [describe or paste your best post]
Client results: [describe your strongest case study or testimonial]
Primary CTA: [what action you most want visitors to take]

RULES:
- Slot 1 must be your highest-converting lead magnet or CTA
- Slot 2 must build credibility: case study, result, or testimonial
- Slot 3 must demonstrate expertise: best content piece
- Slot 4 can be secondary CTA or additional social proof
- Write a 1–2 sentence description for each slot explaining what to include and why
- Output: Slot 1 to 4 with recommendation and rationale
OUTPUT: A four-slot Featured section plan with rationale for each placement
Worked example · Meridian Advisory Group

From a CV-style profile to 14 inbound messages in 3 weeks

A B2B management consulting firm doing €4M in annual revenue. Before running these prompts, the founder's LinkedIn headline read:

"Managing Director | Strategy Consultant | Helping businesses grow"

"Most consulting founders are their own best salesperson. That's not a strength. It's a ceiling. If every deal needs you in the room, your revenue stops when you do."
20 minutes.

Profile updated on a Tuesday afternoon. By the end of that week, three ideal-fit founders had sent connection requests referencing his headline directly.

Within three weeks: 14 inbound messages, 6 discovery calls booked, 2 proposals sent.

What to do right now

  4. Run Prompt 4 and redesign your Featured section. Slot 1 should link directly to your best lead magnet.
