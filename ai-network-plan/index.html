The problem

Why most founders never build the network they need

Building a LinkedIn network of ICPs sounds straightforward. Connect with the right people, engage with their content, stay visible. Most founders know the theory. Very few have a system.

Without a structured plan, network building becomes reactive. You connect with whoever crosses your feed. You engage when you feel inspired. You make progress in bursts and lose momentum between them. Six months in, you have 200 random connections and no clear pipeline.

The difference between a network that produces pipeline and one that does not is not effort. It is architecture. This roadmap gives you that architecture.

The Network Illusion: most founders believe their network is bigger than it is useful.

A large connection count that does not include your ICP is a vanity metric. 500 connected ICPs who know your name and your thinking is a pipeline asset.

01

The 90-Day Network Architect

Builds your full personalised network growth plan across three phases

What you're doing: Creating the full 90-day roadmap. Run this once at the start. It produces a phased plan that tells you exactly what to do each week, how many connections to target, and what your engagement rhythm should look like.

ROLE: B2B growth strategist specialising in LinkedIn network architecture for founder-led businesses.

TASK: Build a 90-day ICP network growth plan using the inputs below.

INPUTS:
ICP profile: [paste your full ICP profile from Resource 01]
Current LinkedIn connections: [total number, rough ICP percentage]
Available time per week: [hours per week you can commit to LinkedIn outreach and engagement]
Current content status: [posting regularly / occasionally / not yet]
Starting goal: [number of connected ICPs you want by day 90]

OUTPUT a 3-phase plan with:
- Phase 1 (Days 1 to 30): Weekly targets, key actions, connection sources to focus on
- Phase 2 (Days 31 to 60): Weekly targets, engagement rhythm, relationship progression criteria
- Phase 3 (Days 61 to 90): Weekly targets, warm network activation, inbound conversation triggers
- One weekly non-negotiable action per phase
- A simple scoring system to assess network quality week by week

RULES:
- Be specific. Give targets, not advice.
- Assume no paid advertising, no cold email, no purchased lists
- Base connection rate on realistic acceptance rates (50 to 65% for personalised requests)
- Output: a structured 3-phase plan the founder can start on Monday
OUTPUT: A personalised 90-day network plan with weekly targets, actions, and a quality scoring system
02

The Weekly Network Sprint

Converts your 90-day plan into a focused weekly action list

What you're doing: Translating the 90-day plan into a specific this-week action list. Run this every Monday. It tells you exactly who to target, how many requests to send, and what to prioritise based on where you are in the plan.

ROLE: B2B network strategist. You help founders execute their weekly ICP connection plan without losing momentum.

TASK: Generate a focused weekly action list based on the inputs below.

INPUTS:
Current phase: [Phase 1 / 2 / 3]
Week number: [e.g. Week 4]
Connections made so far: [total connected ICPs to date]
Last week's results: [connections sent / accepted / conversations opened]
Current blockers or observations: [anything that worked well or did not]

OUTPUT:
- This week's connection target (specific number)
- Top 5 ICP account types to prioritise this week
- Weekly engagement minimum (comments per day, connection requests per day)
- One relationship to advance this week (based on previous engagement)
- One adjustment based on last week's data

RULES:
- Be direct. Give numbers and actions, not suggestions.
- Keep outputs actionable. No strategy, only tactics.
- Output: a weekly action list the founder can start using immediately
OUTPUT: A weekly action list with specific targets, account priorities, and one key adjustment
03

The Network Quality Scorer

Assesses whether your growing network is building toward pipeline or just growing in size

What you're doing: Checking the quality of your network growth, not just the quantity. Connection count is not the goal. A warm network of connected ICPs who engage with your content and reply to your messages is. Run this check every four weeks.

ROLE: B2B pipeline strategist. You assess network quality, not just size.

TASK: Score the network growth below and identify whether it is on track to produce pipeline.

INPUTS:
Total new connections this month: [number]
Estimated ICP match rate: [percentage who match your ICP profile]
Engagement rate from new connections: [percentage who liked, commented, or replied to anything]
Warm conversations opened: [number of meaningful DM exchanges with new connections]
Connection requests sent vs accepted: [sent / accepted]

OUTPUT:
- Overall network quality score out of 10
- What the data says about the quality of connections being made
- The one metric most in need of improvement
- Two specific actions to improve quality over the next 30 days

RULES:
- Be direct. Give a score and a verdict.
- Focus on pipeline potential, not social metrics
- Output: a scored assessment with two actionable improvements
OUTPUT: A network quality score with two specific actions to improve it
Worked example · Meridian Advisory Group

From 180 random connections to 422 connected ICPs in 90 days

A B2B management consulting firm doing €4M in annual revenue. They had 180 LinkedIn connections, most of them former colleagues and peers. Almost none matched their ICP: founders of €2M to €8M professional services businesses.

They ran Prompt 1 on a Monday morning, inputting their ICP profile, a 20-hour-per-month availability, and a target of 400 connected ICPs by day 90.

Phase breakdown produced by Prompt 1

PhaseWeekly targetNon-negotiable actionResult
Phase 1 (Days 1–30)15 connection requests per week, targeting founder profiles onlyRun the ICP Activity Scanner every Monday. Prioritise new posts over passive profiles.87 new ICP connections by day 30
Phase 2 (Days 31–60)20 connection requests per week, plus 3 to 5 daily comments on ICP postsSend one warm follow-up message per week to the 5 highest-engagement connections from Phase 1.194 ICP connections by day 60
Phase 3 (Days 61–90)25 connection requests per week, plus first LinkedIn post publishedTrack which ICP connections engage with the post. These are the warm leads to prioritise for outreach.422 ICP connections by day 90

Network Quality Score at day 90 (Prompt 3 output)

Score: 7.5 / 10. ICP match rate: 81%. Engagement rate from new connections: 34%. Warm conversations opened: 22. The one metric to improve is DM response rate. Two actions: introduce a value-first message to new connections within 48 hours of acceptance, and identify the 10 highest-engagement ICPs for a direct outreach test in month four.

What to do right now
  1. Run Prompt 1 today. Have your ICP Profile from Resource 01 ready to paste in. This is the one-time setup that drives everything else.
  2. Set your 90-day start date. Block 15 minutes every Monday to run the Weekly Network Sprint. This is the only recurring commitment the plan requires.
  3. Score your existing connections before you start. Knowing how many are true ICPs tells you how far you actually are from your target.
  4. Run Prompt 3 at the end of weeks 4, 8, and 12. Quality check at each phase transition. Adjust before momentum is lost.
  5. When conversations open, move them to the AI Connection Request Framework (Resource 04) follow-up sequence. The network is the start, not the end.
