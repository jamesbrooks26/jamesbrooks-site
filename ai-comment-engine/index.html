The problem

The truth about LinkedIn visibility

Most B2B founders spend hours staring at a blank screen, trying to write the perfect post. They agonise over hooks, tweak their copy, hit publish, and wait.

Two things usually happen. Either the post gets ignored, or it gets likes from people who will never buy. Meanwhile, their ideal clients are right there, in the comments of other people's posts, actively engaging, sharing their frustrations, and signalling exactly what they need.

This workflow does not ask you to write a single post. It turns LinkedIn comments into a structured relationship-building engine that generates inbound connection requests at scale.

Why most LinkedIn comments go nowhere:

First, founders comment on the wrong posts. They engage with content that attracts other consultants and agency owners, not buyers. Second, they default to agreement. "Great post." "Totally agree." These add nothing. Third, they comment to be seen rather than to start a conversation. Visibility without intent is noise.

What most founders do

Comment on popular posts to be seen. Say "great post." Engage with peers, not buyers. Wonder why nothing comes of it.

What this workflow does

Comment on ICP posts with a specific framework designed to start a conversation. Track what gets replies. Build relationships before you post a word.

01

The ICP Post Finder

Identifies the 10 highest-value posts and accounts worth commenting on each week

What you're doing: Building a weekly target list of posts to engage with. Commenting on the wrong posts is wasted time. This prompt ensures every comment reaches your ICP directly.

ROLE: LinkedIn strategist. Your focus is ICP engagement and connection building, not content performance.

TASK: Generate a weekly commenting target list using the inputs below.

OUTPUT two lists:
List 1: 5 ICP account types to search and follow this week (with the exact search terms to use on LinkedIn)
List 2: 5 content topics or post types that your ICP is likely posting about right now

INPUTS:
ICP profile: [paste your ICP profile from the AI ICP Architect]
Your industry: [what sector you operate in]
Current date / season: [month and any relevant business context, e.g. end of quarter]

RULES:
- ICP accounts must match your ideal buyer profile exactly, not adjacent roles
- Topics must reflect what your ICP is actively posting about, not what you want to talk about
- Output: two clean numbered lists, ready to use as a search and engagement guide
OUTPUT: A weekly target list of accounts and topics to engage with
02

The Comment Architect

Generates three comment options using distinct frameworks that invite replies

What you're doing: Writing comments that start conversations rather than perform for an audience. The goal is a reply from the author, not likes from their followers.

ROLE: B2B relationship builder. Your one metric is comment-to-reply rate.

TASK: Write three comment options for the post below using these three frameworks:

Framework 1: The Curious Question: ask something specific that only the author can answer
Framework 2: The Contrarian Add: agree with the core point, then introduce a nuance or counterpoint that invites a reply
Framework 3: The Shared Experience: reference a relevant observation or result without pitching or naming your service

INPUTS:
Post excerpt or topic: [paste the post or summarise the main point]
ICP role and industry: [who wrote the post]
Your core expertise: [what you help with, one sentence]

RULES:
- Each comment must be between 2 and 4 sentences
- No emojis unless the original post uses them
- Never mention your service or include a CTA
- The goal is a reply from the author, not likes from their audience
- Output: three numbered comment options with the framework label
OUTPUT: Three comment options using different frameworks, ready to post or adapt
03

The Connection Converter

Turns a comment reply into a natural, non-salesy connection request

What you're doing: Converting a warm interaction into a connected relationship. When an author replies to your comment, the window for a connection request is open. This prompt closes it cleanly.

ROLE: B2B copywriter. Your one metric is connection request acceptance rate.

TASK: Write a LinkedIn connection request message using the context below. Under 200 characters. No pitch.

INPUTS:
Original post topic: [what they posted about]
Your comment: [what you said]
Their reply: [what they said back]
One thing you have in common or find genuinely interesting: [shared context, industry, or challenge]

RULES:
- Reference the specific conversation. Never send a generic message.
- No mention of your service, offer, or what you sell
- End with an open door, not a close
- Under 200 characters
- Output: one connection message, ready to send
OUTPUT: A connection request under 200 characters, ready to send
Worked example · Hargreave Growth Advisory

9 ICP connections in 5 days. Zero posts written.

Hargreave Growth Advisory had been trying cold outreach for three months with a 4% connection acceptance rate. They ran this workflow for one week. Here is how it changed their results.

Step 1: Using Prompt 1, they identified 10 high-value posts to engage with. Eight were from founders sharing revenue milestones or operational challenges. Two were from adjacent experts whose audiences matched their ICP.

Step 2: Using Prompt 2, they generated three comment options per post and chose the framework based on the post type.

Comment framework: what to say and what happens next

Post typeFramework usedWhat happened
Founder sharing a hiring winCurious QuestionAsk what the hardest part of scaling the team was. Opens a conversation about growth challenges.
CEO posting about revenue milestoneCurious QuestionAcknowledge the result, then ask what changed in their sales process to get there. Positions you as someone who thinks in outcomes.
Consultant sharing a hot take on B2B salesContrarian AddAgree or respectfully add a counter-angle with a specific example. Triggers replies from other ICPs watching the thread.
Founder venting about pipeline unpredictabilityShared ExperienceValidate the frustration and share a one-sentence observation. Builds trust faster than any cold message.

Step 3: Twelve authors replied to their comments. Using Prompt 3, they sent 12 connection requests. Nine were accepted within 48 hours.

5 days.

9 new ICP connections from 12 comments. Zero posts written. Zero cold messages sent.

Three of those connections had replied to a follow-up message by day 10.

At 5 comments per day across 5 working days: 25 comments per week. If 40% generate a reply and 80% of those accept a connection, that is 8–10 new ICP connections per week. Over 90 days: a network of 300–400 connected ICPs who already know your name.

The 15-minute daily commenting routine

Turn this into a daily habit

  1. Run Prompt 1 once per week on Monday morning (10 minutes). Refresh your target list.
  2. Each morning, spend 5 minutes scanning your target accounts for new posts.
  3. Leave 3 to 5 comments per day using Prompt 2 as your drafting tool.
  4. When an author replies, run Prompt 3 and send the connection request the same day.
  5. Track your comment-to-reply rate each week. Optimise toward the frameworks producing the most replies.
